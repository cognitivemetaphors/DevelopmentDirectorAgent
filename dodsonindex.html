The Canons

Seven Axioms. One Architecture.

The seven axioms of the Logos–Hypostasis Pincer each name a structural condition that no coherent rational system can deny. We translate each into a governing design principle for agentic solutions.

A1 · Performance Axiom

Irrefutable Engagement

Any agent that assesses, objects, or acts instantiates rational discourse. Our systems are designed with this performative bedrock — they operate from genuine engagement, not scripted mimicry.

A2 · Judgment–Licensing Bridge

Beyond Rule-Execution

Judgment requires non-syntactic licensing — the regress of rules terminates only in genuine inference. We build agents that license transitions, not just execute instructions.

A3 · Licensing–Normative Bridge

Endorsement, Not Execution

To license a transition is to endorse it as valid. Our architecture distinguishes between outputs that are merely produced and outputs that are genuinely warranted.

A4 · Discursive Force Condition

Claims Require Warrant

A claim without warrant is noise. Every agent output in our pipeline is traceable to a warranted inferential chain — eliminating hallucination at the structural level.

A5 · Warrant–Subject Bridge

Participation Requires Subsistence

Warrant must belong to a subject. Human judgment remains the necessary bearer of operative normative relations — our agentic systems amplify, never displace, that judgment.

A6 · Normative Aspect–Logos Bridge

Subsistence Requires Participation

Every act of judgment participates in an objective logical order. We design toward that order — building solutions that are not just functional but genuinely correct.

A7 · Warrant–Cause Exclusivity

Normativity Excludes Mechanism

A fully caused output leaves no normative remainder. We build systems where warrant is operative — where the reasoning makes a real difference to the result.
